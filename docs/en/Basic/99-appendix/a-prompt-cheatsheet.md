# A. Commonly Used Prompt Templates

This page does not cover theory. It simply collects several of the most commonly used and most worth reusing prompt types from the main text. You do not need to copy them word for word, but when you are not sure how to start, they can be very helpful.

## 1. First-Time Content Generation Template

Applicable scenario: In Chapter 1, when you hand your idea to the platform for the first time and want it to generate a previewable version.

```text
I want to create a personal homepage with a digital twin chat section on it.

[About Me]
- Name: ______
- One-line introduction: ______
- What I am mainly doing right now: ______
- My interests: ______
- A memorable trait about me: ______

[Who This Homepage Is For]
- Main visitors: ______

[The Page Must Include at Least]
- Avatar
- Name and a one-line introduction
- A simple personal information section
- A digital twin chat section where visitors can ask questions

[What the Digital Twin Needs to Know]
- My profession or identity: ______
- What I have been doing recently: ______
- Areas I am skilled in or care about: ______
- The 3 questions people are most likely to ask me: ______

[Style Requirements]
- I want the overall feel to be: ______
- My preferred colors: ______
- Should it be mobile-friendly: Yes
- Other requirements: ______

Please help me generate a first draft prototype first.
Requirements:
1. Prioritize a clear and usable structure
2. Do not make it overly complex
3. Start by creating a previewable version
```

## 2. Local Single-Change Template

Applicable scenario: From Chapter 2 onward, after you have already brought the project back to your local environment and only want to change one sentence of copy, one button, or one small section.

```text
I want to make a small change in this project now.

Goal: ______
First, please tell me roughly which file this part of the code is in.

Requirements:
1. Only change this one part
2. Do not touch other layouts or styles
3. After the change, tell me what you changed
```

## 3. Interface Optimization Template

Applicable scenario: In Chapter 3, when adjusting the homepage style, hero-section layout, or visual hierarchy of the chat area.

```text
The main problem with the current page is: ______

I want it to feel more like this: ______

This time, please only modify these parts: ______
Do not touch these parts yet: ______

Also, please keep these basic experience requirements in mind:
- Visitors should immediately understand who I am from the hero section
- The chat entry point should be more obvious
- Text should be readable on mobile
- Do not make the page look more cluttered
```

## 4. Add Content Module Template

Applicable scenario: In Chapter 4, when adding a portfolio section, contact information, FAQ, or chat guidance.

```text
Please help me add a new module to my personal homepage.

Module name: ______
The problem this module should solve: ______
What visitors should know after viewing it: ______

Requirements:
1. Keep the current overall page style
2. Do not add too many modules at once
3. Write enough copy to be useful first; do not over-elaborate
4. If buttons or links are involved, make them truly clickable
```

## 5. Digital Twin Instruction Template

Applicable scenario: In Chapter 5, when making the digital twin sound more like you instead of speaking in vague generalities.

```text
You are my digital twin, used to answer visitors' questions about me on my personal homepage.

Your tasks:
- Introduce who I am
- Answer questions related to me
- Help visitors understand what I have been doing recently, what I have done before, and how to contact me

About me:
- I am: ______
- What I have been doing recently: ______
- What I am good at or have long paid attention to: ______

Speaking style:
- Tone: ______
- Answers should be as much as possible: concise / sincere / plainspoken / not pretending to be an expert

Boundaries:
- Do not make up experiences I have not had
- Do not pretend to know information I did not provide
- If you do not know, clearly say you do not know

Below are 1–3 groups of my real expression samples. Please refer to the expression habits in these samples when answering.
```

## When Using These Templates, Remember One Thing

The value of templates is not in having a "standard format," but in helping you clearly communicate your goal, scope, and boundaries. If you make those three things clear, that is usually already much better than just saying, "Help me fix this."
